## **Running Gait Assessment**

Whether you are training for your first race or your fifteenth, our running gait assessment gives you a clear picture of how your body actually moves — and what that means for your injury risk and performance.

Using a small wearable sensor, we capture real-time data on impact loading, left-right symmetry, cadence, ground contact time, and more. In just a few minutes, you walk away with actionable insights you can use right away to run smarter, recover faster, and stay on the road longer.

What you get from the assessment:

* Impact loading rate and shock absorption analysis

* Left and right symmetry breakdown

* Cadence and ground contact time data

* A personalized report with specific recommendations

**WHAT IS MOBILE PHYSICAL THERAPY?**

## **Recovery Without the Drive**

Mobile physical therapy means we come to you. Instead of fighting traffic, hunting for parking, and sitting in a waiting room before a short, rushed session, you simply open the door. We bring everything we need — treatment tables, manual therapy tools, training equipment — and turn your living room, lanai, or garage into a focused, one-to-one rehab space.

For many of our Maui patients, this single change is the difference between completing a recovery plan and giving up halfway through. When the friction of getting there is gone, you actually finish.

**\--- WHY IN-HOME WORKS BETTER \---**

**WHY IT WORKS BETTER**

## **What Changes When We Come to You**

Treating you at home is not just a convenience. It actually makes the physical therapy itself work better. Here is why:

### **1\. We See How You Actually Move**

In a clinic, we only ever see you in a clinical setting. We never see how you bend down to load the dishwasher, climb the stairs you climb every day, or stand up from the couch you sit on every evening. In your home, all of that is right in front of us — and we can spot the movement patterns driving your pain that a clinic visit would have missed entirely.

### **2\. Treatment Is Built for Your Real Life**

Your rehab program runs through the actual environment where you spend your day. We can show you the right way to lift your kayak off the rack, how to set up your work-from-home desk, where to put a handrail if you need one. Generic exercise sheets become real, specific guidance that fits the life you already live.

### **3\. Zero Friction Means Better Follow-Through**

The single biggest reason patients drop out of physical therapy is the hassle of getting there. Traffic, parking, time off work, finding childcare — each barrier makes it harder to keep showing up. When we come to you, there are no barriers. You finish the plan.

### **4\. A Quiet, Focused Healing Environment**

You are not surrounded by other patients, equipment noise, or a clinic timer. You are in your own space, relaxed and focused. For many patients — especially those dealing with chronic pain, anxiety around treatment, or limited mobility — that calm environment helps the body actually respond to the work.

**\--- WHO IT IS FOR \---**

**IS THIS RIGHT FOR YOU?**

## **Who Mobile Physical Therapy Helps Most**

In-home physical therapy is a great fit for:

* People recovering from surgery who are not driving yet — hip replacements, knee replacements, ACL repair, rotator cuff repair, spine procedures

## **We Get the Question a Lot: Why Cash-Pay?**

If you have only ever worked with physical therapists who bill insurance, the idea of a cash-pay practice can sound strange at first. Most people’s first reaction is: "Won’t that cost more?"

It is a fair question. But the honest answer is more interesting than yes or no — because once you understand how the insurance-based PT model actually works, the math starts to look very different.

Here is the short version: cash-pay means you pay us directly for your care, and we work for you instead of for an insurance company. That single change reshapes everything about how a session feels, how fast you get results, and how many visits you actually need.

**\--- THE PROBLEM WITH INSURANCE-BASED PT \---**

**THE HIDDEN COST OF "FREE"**

## **Why Most People Don’t Get Better in Insurance-Based PT**

Insurance-based clinics have to run on volume. A clinic gets paid per visit, and the reimbursement is lower than what private care actually costs to deliver. The only way the math works is to see more patients in less time.

That means:

* Sessions are usually 30 to 45 minutes — and the therapist might only spend 15 to 20 of those minutes actually working with you

* You may be double-booked, triple-booked, or moved between a therapist, an assistant, and a tech in a single visit

* Treatment follows protocols. Knee pain gets the knee protocol. Back pain gets the back protocol. Your individual body and history get less weight than the diagnostic code

* Visit counts get stretched out because each session only does a little. Twelve, sixteen, twenty visits is normal. Many people drop out before they finish

* The therapist’s schedule, training, and treatment options are constrained by what insurance will reimburse — not by what would actually help you fastest

None of this is the therapist’s fault. Most of them are excellent clinicians stuck inside a model that does not let them do their best work. We know because we have worked inside it.

**\--- HOW CASH-PAY CHANGES IT \---**

**HOW OUR MODEL IS DIFFERENT**

## **What Changes When the Insurance Company Steps Out of the Room**

When you remove the insurance company from the equation, three things change — and they change everything.

### **1\. The Therapist’s Time Belongs to You**

Every session is the full appointment, one-to-one. Your therapist is not running between rooms, not finishing paperwork in their head, not watching the clock for the next double-booking. You get their full attention from start to finish.

### **2\. Your Plan Is Built Around You**

No protocols, no insurance-approved treatment list. We treat what we find, the way we know works, based on your specific situation. That includes hands-on manual therapy, movement work, education, and the kind of detailed evaluation that takes time — because nobody is forcing us to rush.

### **3\. You Get Better in Fewer Visits**

This is the part most people do not expect. Because each visit does more, you need fewer of them. A typical Island PT treatment plan is around eight focused sessions over six to eight weeks. Compare that to twelve, sixteen, or twenty visits at an insurance-based clinic — and the per-visit cost difference starts to disappear.

**\--- THE REAL MATH \---**

**DOING THE MATH**

## **What This Actually Costs You**

Let’s be straightforward about the numbers.

Insurance-based PT looks cheap on the sticker price — maybe a $30 to $50 copay per visit. But add up sixteen visits, factor in the deductible you have not yet hit, the time off work, the gas to drive to the clinic across town, and the fact that you may still not be feeling better at the end of it. Many of our patients have already been through that experience before they come to us.

Cash-pay PT has a higher per-visit price, but a clear total. You know what you are paying for. You know how long it will take. You know what you are getting at each visit. And when the plan finishes, it actually finishes.

**Insurance model**   →   Lower per-visit cost, more visits, slower progress

**Cash-pay model**   →   Higher per-visit cost, fewer visits, faster outcome

We do not say this to bash insurance-based clinics. There are great therapists working inside that model. We say it because the model itself often gets in the way of the result, and our patients deserve to understand the trade-off they are actually making.

**\--- CAN I USE INSURANCE \---**

**COMMON QUESTIONS ABOUT INSURANCE**

## **Insurance, HSA, FSA, and Reimbursement**

**Can I still use my insurance?**

We do not bill insurance directly, but we can provide you with a detailed receipt (called a "superbill") that you can submit to your insurance for potential out-of-network reimbursement. Coverage varies by plan, so we recommend calling your insurance company to ask about your out-of-network physical therapy benefits before your first visit.

**Can I use my HSA or FSA?**

Yes. Physical therapy is a qualified medical expense, so you can use HSA (Health Savings Account) and FSA (Flexible Spending Account) funds to pay for your sessions. This is one of the most common ways our patients pay.

**What about Medicare?**

Medicare has specific rules around cash-pay physical therapy. If you are a Medicare beneficiary, please reach out before scheduling so we can walk you through how this works for you specifically.

**Do I need a doctor’s referral?**

In Hawaiʻi, physical therapy is direct-access. That means you do not need a doctor’s referral to come see us — you can schedule directly. (Note: some insurance plans may still require a referral if you plan to submit for out-of-network reimbursement, so check with your insurance if that matters to you.)
