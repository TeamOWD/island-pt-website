01 / WHAT IS IT

Recovery Without the Drive.

What Is Mobile Physical Therapy?

Mobile physical therapy means we come to you.

Instead of fighting traffic, hunting for parking, and sitting in a waiting room before a short, rushed session, you simply open the door. We bring everything we need — treatment tables, manual therapy tools, training equipment — and turn your living room, lanai, or garage into a focused, one-to-one rehab space.

For many of our Maui patients, this single change is the difference between completing a recovery plan and giving up halfway through. When the friction of getting there is gone, you actually get the work done.

02 / WHY IT WORKS

What Changes When We Come to You.

01.

We See How You Actually Move.

In a clinic, we only ever see you in a clinical setting. We never see how you bend down to load the dishwasher, climb the stairs you climb every day, or stand up from the couch you sit on every evening. In your home, all of that is right in front of us — and we can spot the movement patterns driving your pain that a clinic visit would have missed entirely.

02.

Treatment Is Built for Your Real Life.

Your rehab program runs through the actual environment where you spend your day. We can show you the right way to lift your kayak off the rack, how to set up your work-from-home desk, where to put a handrail if you need one. Generic exercise sheets become real, specific guidance that fits the life you already live.

03.

Zero Friction Means Better Follow-Through.

The single biggest reason patients drop out of physical therapy is the hassle of getting there. Traffic, parking, time off work, finding childcare — each barrier makes it harder to keep showing up. When we come to you, there are no barriers. You finish the plan.

04.

A Quiet, Focused Healing Environment.

You are not surrounded by other patients, equipment noise, or a clinic timer. You are in your own space, relaxed and focused. For many patients — especially those dealing with chronic pain, anxiety around treatment, or limited mobility — that calm environment helps the body actually respond to the work.

03 / IS THIS RIGHT FOR YOU?

Who Mobile Physical Therapy Helps Most.

In-home physical therapy is a great fit for:

  • People recovering from surgery who are not driving yet — hip replacements, knee replacements, ACL repair, rotator cuff repair, spine procedures
  • Older adults focused on balance, fall prevention, and staying independent at home
  • Patients with chronic back, neck, or joint pain who find clinic visits aggravate things rather than help
  • Busy professionals and parents who simply cannot afford to lose two hours every visit to drive time
  • Athletes whose training and rehab work happens at home, in the water, or on the trails — where we can actually meet them
  • Anyone who has tried clinic-based PT before and never made it through the full plan

If any of that sounds like you, mobile physical therapy is probably worth a conversation.

04 / EQUIPMENT

We Bring the Clinic to You.

Equipment & Expertise

Some patients wonder if in-home physical therapy means a stripped-down version of what they would get at a clinic. The opposite is true. We arrive with everything we need to run a complete session.

  • A portable treatment table for hands-on manual therapy
  • Resistance bands, weights, balance tools, and movement-assessment equipment
  • Manual therapy tools — mobilization, soft-tissue work, joint care
  • Educational materials and a personalized at-home program built around what you already have

Most importantly, you are getting the same Doctor of Physical Therapy with the same advanced training you would get in a clinic — with more of their attention, not less.

06 / HOW IT WORKS

What to Expect From Your Home Visit.

i. Step 01

Before We Arrive.

Once you schedule your first visit, we will let you know roughly what to expect, what to wear, and any small things to have ready. A clear floor space about the size of a yoga mat works great. No prep is required — we handle the rest.

ii. Step 02

The Evaluation.

Your first appointment is a comprehensive evaluation. We will talk through your history, examine how you move in your own environment, and identify the underlying causes of your pain or limitation.

iii. Step 03

The Treatment Plan.

Most plans run six to eight weeks — typically around eight one-to-one sessions in your home. Each session is the full appointment, focused entirely on you, with hands-on treatment plus targeted movement work.

iv. Step 04

Between Visits.

You will leave each visit with a clear, manageable home program designed around your real life. No printed sheets of generic exercises — just a few specific, well-coached movements that move the needle.

08 / FAQ

Mobile Physical Therapy: Frequently Asked Questions.

How much space do I need in my home?

Very little. Anywhere with a clear floor area roughly the size of a yoga mat works. We can set up in your living room, lanai, garage, bedroom — wherever you have a comfortable spot.

Do I need to provide any equipment?

No. We bring everything. If you happen to have specific items at home (a yoga mat, a chair, stairs we can use), we will work them in but nothing is required from you.

How long is each visit?

Most sessions are 60 minutes, and the time is entirely focused on you. No double-booking, no interruptions.

How is mobile PT priced compared to clinic PT?

Our mobile rate reflects the travel time and the fact that each visit is delivered one-to-one. We are happy to walk you through the pricing on a consultation call, and we are transparent about it up front so there are no surprises.

Can I use insurance for mobile physical therapy?

We are a cash-pay practice, but we can provide a detailed receipt (superbill) for you to submit to your insurance for potential out-of-network reimbursement. HSA and FSA funds can also be used. See our Why Cash-Pay page for the full breakdown.

How quickly can I get started?

We aim to schedule your first visit within a week of your initial inquiry. Reach out and we will find a time that works for you.
